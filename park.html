📋 The Situation

Your neighbourhood park has a severe litter problem (75%). The government already installed more bins, but residents keep complaining. You've been appointed to lead a 8-month cleanup initiative.

Your target: bring litter below 15% AND keep citizen happiness above 60%. Fail either and you won't get the top grades.

👥 Three Stakeholders

Government

Policy, enforcement, infrastructure

🙋

Citizens

Habits, volunteering, ownership

🤝

Community Groups

Campaigns, gotong royong, partnerships

⚙️ How It Works

New actions unlock over time. The game has 3 phases — early actions are basic, mid-game unlocks stronger options, and late-game has powerful but expensive moves. You won't see the same choices every month.

Actions have cooldowns. You can't spam the same action every month — plan ahead.

Public Opinion matters. If it drops too low, your budget gets slashed. Keep the people on your side.

Crises will hit. Random events disrupt your plans. Some months will force you into tough trade-offs.

Months 1–3

Foundation phase. Basic actions, build groundwork.

Months 4–6

Escalation. Stronger options, bigger events.

Months 7–8

Final push. Power moves & last chances.

🏆 Grading System

Your final grade is based on Litter Level (lower = better), Happiness (higher = better), Public Opinion, Budget Efficiency, and Stakeholder Balance. Meeting both targets (litter ≤15% AND happiness ≥60%) gives a bonus.
